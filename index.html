Orthopaedics

Orthopedic physiotherapy treatment involves the use of physical therapy techniques to treat musculoskeletal conditions, injuries, and disorders. This may include exercises to improve strength and flexibility, manual therapy to improve joint mobility and reduce pain, and education to help patients manage their condition and prevent further injury.

Sports injuries

Sports injuries can be effectively treated with physiotherapy, which involves a range of techniques to help reduce pain, inflammation and muscle tension, and promote healing and recovery. Some of the common physiotherapy treatments for sports injuries include manual therapy, exercise therapy, electrotherapy, and hot and cold therapy.

Neurology

Neurology physiotherapy treatment focuses on restoring and improving the function of the nervous system and muscles. This type of therapy uses various techniques, such as exercise, manual therapy, and functional training, to help patients with neurological conditions regain their mobility, strength, and independence.

Soft tissue injuries

Soft tissue physiotherapy treatment involves the assessment, diagnosis, and management of soft tissue injuries and disorders through various manual techniques, such as massage, stretching, mobilization, and exercise prescription.Soft tissue physiotherapy can be effective for a variety of conditions, including sports injuries, chronic pain, and postural imbalances.
